Vogue CS

Wireframe and prototype
of ontent based web

Vogue is an international fashion and lifestyle magazine published monthly in more than twenty-five countries around the world. Each country creates its own content - both printed and online.

This year Vogue came to Czechia and Slovakia and they chose the agency I’m working for to help with an (almost) impossible task - to design, develop and launch a website for Vogue CS in four months.

My role

I was the only UX designer on this project. My task was to create the specifications for the minimum viable product based on the client’s requirements and technical restrictions and to supervise the website design and development.

Design and research

My first task was to collect requirements from the client, do research and create the first specification (wireframe in Axure) for the web structure. I then reviewed it with developers to see what was possible within the deadline. I processed more detailed wireframes and I ran a workshop with the client in which the whole structure was evaluated.

Team collaboration

Wireframes were just the beginning of my work. I collaborated closely with the whole team on a daily basis including regular meetings with the graphic designer to make every detail perfect; daily communication with the client to answer all his/her questions; processing of new requirements; meetings with the development team to make sure they are informed of the latest changes; and checking the functionality of what had been implemented.

Launch

We successfully launched the website on time. The website exceeded all KPIs in terms of visits, magazine subscriptions, newsletter subscriptions, and returning visitors.

Personal takeaways

When dealing with an ad-hoc and constantly changing project like this one, the best way to be efficient is to be in constant communication with the developers and designers, so that all features are implemented effectively.
